# CloudSewa VPS Server Policy

## 1. VPS Provisioning

After successful payment, VPS is normally provisioned within 5 to 30 minutes.

Provisioning may take longer if manual fraud review is required, payment verification is pending, selected location has low capacity, custom OS image is requested, additional managed setup is requested, or control panel license activation is pending.

If VPS is not provisioned within 2 hours after confirmed payment, customer may open a support ticket.

## 2. Supported Operating Systems

CloudSewa VPS may support Ubuntu LTS, Debian, AlmaLinux, Rocky Linux, CentOS Stream, Fedora Server, Windows Server for selected plans, and custom ISO for advanced customers.

Available OS images may change depending on virtualization platform and security support status.

## 3. Root Access

Unmanaged VPS customers receive root or administrator access.

Customers are responsible for keeping credentials safe.

CloudSewa does not store customer root password after initial provisioning unless the customer uses managed support and explicitly shares access.

If customer loses root password, options include reset password from panel if supported, boot into rescue mode, reinstall OS, or request managed recovery assistance.

## 4. VPS Reboot Policy

Customers may reboot VPS from the control panel.

CloudSewa may reboot VPS without prior notice only when required for emergency security patching, hypervisor maintenance, network recovery, abuse containment, or infrastructure stability.

For planned maintenance, CloudSewa tries to provide advance notice.

## 5. VPS Suspension

VPS may be suspended due to unpaid invoice, abuse complaint, malware activity, spam sending, phishing, DDoS source activity, law enforcement request, acceptable use violation, excessive resource abuse, or payment dispute or chargeback.

Suspension does not automatically delete data. However, prolonged suspension may lead to termination according to billing policy.

## 6. VPS Termination

VPS may be terminated if invoice remains unpaid after grace period, abuse is severe or unresolved, customer requests cancellation, payment is reversed, service is used for illegal activity, or account verification fails.

Once VPS is terminated, data recovery may not be possible unless backup exists.

## 7. VPS Performance Issues

When customer reports slow VPS, support should check CPU usage, RAM usage, disk I/O, network usage, running processes, application logs, database load, malware signs, external attack traffic, and plan resource limits.

For unmanaged VPS, CloudSewa can confirm infrastructure health, but application-level optimization is customer responsibility.

## 8. IP Address Policy

Each VPS includes at least one public IP address unless stated otherwise.

Additional IPs may require technical justification, additional monthly fee, abuse history review, and regional availability check.

IP address may change during certain migrations, abuse events, or infrastructure recovery unless customer has reserved IP service.

## 9. Bandwidth Policy

Each VPS plan includes a monthly bandwidth allowance.

If customer exceeds bandwidth, speed may be limited, additional bandwidth may be billed, customer may be asked to upgrade plan, or service may continue with reduced speed depending on plan.

Bandwidth is calculated based on inbound and outbound traffic unless plan says otherwise.
