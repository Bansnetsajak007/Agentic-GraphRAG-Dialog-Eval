## 1. Company Overview

PaySathi Wallet is a fictional digital wallet and payment service platform that allows users to make digital payments, transfer wallet balance, pay merchants, load funds, withdraw funds to bank accounts, complete utility payments, verify identity through KYC, and raise support tickets for failed or disputed transactions.

PaySathi provides payment infrastructure between users, merchants, banks, and service providers. The wallet is designed for everyday digital payment use cases such as mobile top-up, internet bill payment, electricity bill payment, QR merchant payment, wallet-to-wallet transfer, wallet-to-bank transfer, bank-to-wallet load, online merchant checkout, and transaction history management.

PaySathi does not manufacture goods, deliver physical products, or directly control third-party merchant services. When a user pays a merchant through PaySathi, PaySathi acts as the payment facilitator. Product delivery, service delivery, cancellation approval, refund eligibility, and order-level responsibility remain with the merchant unless PaySathi directly operates the service.

## 2. Purpose of This Policy

This policy explains how PaySathi handles customer support issues related to failed payments, money deducted but not received, pending transactions, KYC verification, refunds, merchant disputes, wallet limits, transaction verification, suspicious activity, account holds and restrictions, chargeback-style investigation requests, and support ticket escalation.

The goal of this policy is to provide a consistent decision-making framework for customer support agents and to give users clear expectations about what PaySathi can and cannot do.

## 3. Important Definitions

A user is any individual who creates a PaySathi Wallet account.

A verified user is a user whose KYC documents have been reviewed and approved by PaySathi.

An unverified user is a user who has created an account but has not completed KYC verification.

A merchant is a business that accepts PaySathi payments for goods or services.

A transaction is any movement of funds or payment attempt through PaySathi.

A successful transaction is a transaction where PaySathi receives confirmation from the payment processor, merchant system, bank, or service provider.

A failed transaction is a transaction where the payment attempt does not complete successfully and the amount is not settled to the receiver.

A pending transaction is a transaction where PaySathi has not yet received final success or failure confirmation.

A reversed transaction is a transaction where deducted money is returned to the original funding source or PaySathi wallet balance.

A refund is a return of money after a successful payment, usually based on merchant approval, service failure, duplicate payment, or PaySathi investigation.

A dispute is a formal support request where the user claims that a transaction was incorrect, unauthorized, not delivered, duplicated, or not received by the intended party.

KYC means Know Your Customer. It is the identity verification process required before a user can access higher wallet limits or sensitive financial services.

## 4. General Customer Support Principles

PaySathi support agents must follow these principles:

1. Always verify the user before discussing account-sensitive information.
2. Never ask for password, PIN, OTP, full card number, or full bank credentials.
3. Never promise a refund before checking transaction status.
4. Never reverse money manually without approval from the correct operations team.
5. Always explain the current transaction state clearly: successful, failed, pending, reversed, under investigation, or merchant-dependent.
6. Always create or update a support ticket for issues involving deducted money.
7. Always collect proof when the user claims money was deducted from bank or card but not loaded to PaySathi.
8. Always check if the issue belongs to PaySathi, the bank, the merchant, or the external service provider.
9. Always provide expected resolution time.
10. Always escalate suspicious or high-value cases to Risk Operations.

## 5. Account Registration Policy

To create a PaySathi Wallet account, a user must provide active mobile number, full legal name, date of birth, email address if available, device information, and acceptance of PaySathi terms and conditions.

The mobile number is the primary user identifier. A user should not create multiple accounts using different numbers to bypass wallet limits, promotional limits, or compliance restrictions.

PaySathi may restrict accounts that appear to be duplicate, fraudulent, automated, or created using false identity details.

## 6. KYC Verification Policy

KYC verification is required for users who want to access full wallet features, higher transaction limits, wallet-to-bank withdrawals, merchant payments above basic limits, or certain regulated financial services.

### 6.1 Documents Accepted for KYC

PaySathi may accept citizenship certificate, national identity card, passport, driving license, voter identity card if accepted by compliance, and other government-approved documents depending on internal policy.

The user may also be required to submit live selfie, document front image, document back image, permanent address, temporary address, occupation, source of income, nominee or emergency contact if required, and parent or guardian details for eligible minor accounts if applicable.

### 6.2 KYC Review Timeline

Standard KYC review time is 24 to 48 business hours after successful submission.

KYC review may take longer if uploaded document is blurry, name does not match the account name, date of birth is unclear, document number is missing, selfie does not match document photo, document appears expired/edited/cropped/suspicious, the same document is already linked to another PaySathi account, the user submits incomplete address details, or compliance team needs manual verification.

### 6.3 KYC Rejection Reasons

KYC may be rejected for blurry document image, incomplete document upload, mismatched name, mismatched date of birth, fake or altered document, expired document, duplicate document already used by another account, face mismatch between selfie and document, underage user without required guardian process, suspicious activity detected before approval, unsupported document type, or information in the form not matching the uploaded document.

### 6.4 KYC Resubmission

If KYC is rejected, the user may resubmit corrected details. The user must not create a new account to avoid rejection. Repeated submission of fake or edited documents may result in permanent account restriction.

### 6.5 KYC Support Message Example

If a user says:
"mero KYC reject vayo kina?"

Support should answer:
"Tapai ko KYC reject hunu ko reason document blurry, name mismatch, DOB mismatch, duplicate document, or incomplete information huna sakcha. Please PaySathi app ma Profile > KYC Status section check garnu. Tya exact rejection reason dekhincha. Correct document upload garnu bhaye 24 to 48 business hours bhitra review huncha."

## 7. Wallet Transaction Limits

PaySathi applies different transaction limits for verified and unverified users. Limits may change based on regulation, risk category, account age, user behavior, merchant type, or compliance review.

### 7.1 Unverified User Limits

Unverified users may have restricted limits. Example limits:

1. Maximum wallet balance: NPR 5,000.
2. Maximum daily transaction: NPR 5,000.
3. Maximum monthly transaction: NPR 5,000.
4. Wallet-to-bank transfer: Not allowed.
5. High-value merchant payment: Not allowed.
6. Wallet-to-wallet transfer: Limited or unavailable depending on risk rules.

### 7.2 Verified User Limits

Verified users may access higher limits. Example limits:

1. Bank-to-wallet load per transaction: NPR 200,000.
2. Bank-to-wallet load per day: NPR 200,000.
3. Bank-to-wallet load per month: NPR 1,000,000.
4. Wallet-to-bank transfer per transaction: NPR 50,000.
5. Wallet-to-bank transfer per day: NPR 200,000.
6. Wallet-to-bank transfer per month: NPR 1,000,000.
7. Wallet-to-wallet transfer per transaction: NPR 25,000.
8. Wallet-to-wallet transfer per day: NPR 50,000.
9. Wallet-to-wallet transfer per month: NPR 500,000.

These limits are internal sample limits for research data generation. PaySathi may revise limits anytime based on compliance, regulatory instructions, fraud control, or product updates.

### 7.3 Limit Exceeded Cases

If a user sees "limit exceeded," support must check whether the user is KYC verified, transaction type, per transaction limit, daily limit, monthly limit, wallet balance limit, whether the account is temporarily restricted, and whether the merchant has a lower transaction limit.

Support should not manually bypass limits unless Risk and Compliance teams approve.

## 8. Bank-to-Wallet Load Policy

Users may load money to PaySathi from linked banks, mobile banking, internet banking, card, or authorized load partners.

### 8.1 Successful Bank Load

A bank load is considered successful when user's bank confirms debit, PaySathi receives successful payment response, wallet balance is updated, and transaction receipt is generated.

### 8.2 Money Deducted from Bank but Not Added to Wallet

This common support case may happen because of network timeout, delayed bank response, duplicate callback issue, processor delay, or reconciliation mismatch.

If the bank amount is deducted but wallet balance is not updated, the user must provide PaySathi registered mobile number, transaction date and time, bank name, deducted amount, bank reference ID or transaction ID, screenshot of bank debit message or statement, and PaySathi transaction ID if available.

### 8.3 Resolution Timeline

For most bank load mismatch cases:

1. Auto-reversal may happen within 15 minutes to 24 hours.
2. Manual verification may take 1 to 3 business days.
3. Bank-side reconciliation may take up to 5 business days.
4. Complex cases involving third-party processors may take up to 7 business days.

### 8.4 Possible Outcomes

After investigation, PaySathi may credit the amount to the user's wallet, confirm that the bank reversed the amount, ask the user to contact the bank if the debit was not settled to PaySathi, keep the ticket open until bank confirmation is received, or escalate to payment operations if the transaction reference exists in PaySathi logs.

### 8.5 Support Message Example

If a user says:
"bank bata paisa katyo tara PaySathi ma load vayena"

Support should answer:
"Please transaction ko screenshot, bank reference ID, amount, date/time, ani PaySathi registered number pathaunu. Hamro system ma transaction pending cha ki failed cha check garera resolution dinchhau. Usually auto reversal 24 hours bhitra huncha, tara bank reconciliation case ma 3 to 5 business days lagna sakcha."

## 9. Wallet-to-Wallet Transfer Policy

Users may send money from one PaySathi wallet to another PaySathi wallet if both accounts are eligible.

### 9.1 Successful Wallet Transfer

A wallet-to-wallet transfer is successful when sender balance is debited, receiver balance is credited, both users receive transaction receipt, and PaySathi transaction status is marked successful.

### 9.2 Money Sent to Wrong Number

PaySathi cannot automatically reverse a successful wallet-to-wallet transfer only because the sender entered the wrong mobile number.

If money is sent to the wrong number, PaySathi will verify transaction status, may contact the receiver if legally and operationally allowed, may request receiver consent before reversal, cannot guarantee recovery if receiver has already used the money, may temporarily hold the receiver account if fraud is suspected, and may ask the user to submit a written dispute request.

### 9.3 User Responsibility

Users are responsible for checking receiver mobile number, receiver name displayed before confirmation, amount, remarks, and final confirmation screen.

### 9.4 Wrong Transfer Support Message

If a user says:
"galti le arko number ma paisa pathaye"

Support should answer:
"Tapai le successful wallet transfer wrong number ma garnu bhayeko cha bhane PaySathi le direct reverse garna mildaina. Hami receiver lai contact garna sakchhau, tara refund receiver ko consent, balance availability, ani dispute verification ma depend garcha. Please transaction ID, amount, receiver number, date/time pathaunu."

## 10. Wallet-to-Bank Transfer Policy

Verified users may withdraw wallet balance to supported bank accounts.

### 10.1 Successful Wallet-to-Bank Transfer

A wallet-to-bank transfer is successful when PaySathi debits wallet balance, bank transfer request is accepted, bank credits the user account, and transaction status becomes successful.

### 10.2 Money Deducted but Bank Account Not Credited

If wallet balance is deducted but bank account is not credited, user must wait for the normal processing window, PaySathi must check transfer status, support should not create duplicate transfer if status is pending, PaySathi should reverse amount to wallet if status is failed, and PaySathi must request bank confirmation if bank status is successful but user claims not received.

### 10.3 Resolution Timeline

1. Normal transfer: Instant to 30 minutes.
2. Delayed transfer: Up to 24 hours.
3. Bank reconciliation: 1 to 3 business days.
4. Complex bank dispute: Up to 7 business days.

### 10.4 Wrong Bank Account Details

If the user enters wrong bank account details and the bank accepts the transfer, PaySathi cannot guarantee reversal. PaySathi may assist by raising a bank trace request.

## 11. Merchant Payment Policy

Users can pay merchants through QR, online checkout, payment link, invoice, or in-app service.

### 11.1 Successful Merchant Payment

A merchant payment is successful when user wallet or funding source is debited, merchant receives payment confirmation, PaySathi records the payment as successful, and receipt is generated.

### 11.2 Payment Successful but Merchant Says Not Received

If the user paid successfully but merchant says payment was not received, user must provide transaction ID, support must check merchant settlement status, support must verify whether payment was made to the correct merchant ID, PaySathi may send payment confirmation to merchant, Merchant Operations may contact the merchant, and user should not pay again unless support or merchant confirms failure.

### 11.3 Merchant Settlement Delay

Sometimes merchant dashboard update may be delayed due to network, settlement batch delay, or merchant-side sync issue. If PaySathi status is successful, merchant should provide service after verification.

### 11.4 Merchant Refund Responsibility

If the user requests refund because of product cancellation, wrong product, damaged item, unavailable service, duplicate booking, or service dissatisfaction, the refund usually depends on merchant policy.

PaySathi can assist with payment proof and dispute communication, but PaySathi does not decide merchant refund eligibility unless payment failed technically, duplicate payment occurred, merchant was charged but order was not created due to PaySathi system error, merchant is confirmed fraudulent or suspended, or PaySathi campaign terms promise direct refund protection.

## 12. Refund Policy

Refunds are handled based on transaction type.

### 12.1 Refund for Failed Transaction

If a transaction failed and money was deducted, PaySathi will reverse the amount after confirming that the receiver or merchant did not receive funds.

Expected timeline:

1. Wallet reversal: 15 minutes to 24 hours.
2. Bank reversal: 1 to 5 business days.
3. Card reversal: 3 to 10 business days.
4. Merchant settlement reversal: 1 to 7 business days.

### 12.2 Refund for Duplicate Payment

If the user pays twice for the same order, user must provide both transaction IDs, PaySathi checks whether both payments were successful, merchant confirmation may be required, and if duplicate payment is confirmed, one payment may be refunded to wallet or original payment source depending on transaction type.

### 12.3 Refund for Cancelled Order

If the merchant approves cancellation, merchant initiates refund from dashboard or support channel, PaySathi validates refund request, refund is credited to user wallet or original payment source, and user receives refund notification.

### 12.4 Refund for Wrong Mobile Top-Up

If user performs mobile top-up to wrong number and telecom provider has already processed recharge, refund may not be possible. PaySathi may only assist if transaction is pending or failed. User must verify number before confirming top-up. PaySathi is not responsible for wrong number entered by user.

### 12.5 Refund for Utility Bill Payment

If user pays wrong customer ID, wrong account number, or wrong service details, PaySathi will verify whether biller accepted the payment. If biller accepted, refund depends on biller policy. If biller rejected, PaySathi will reverse after confirmation. If customer ID is valid but belongs to another customer, PaySathi cannot guarantee refund.

### 12.6 Non-Refundable Cases

Refund may not be available when user entered wrong receiver details and funds were successfully credited, user shared OTP or PIN and transaction was authorized, merchant delivered service as per order, telecom top-up was successful, utility bill was accepted by biller, promotional cashback expired, user violated PaySathi terms, account is under fraud investigation, or user attempts to abuse refund policy.

## 13. Failed Payment Policy

A payment may fail because of insufficient wallet balance, bank server timeout, merchant server timeout, payment gateway failure, internet connection issue, wrong OTP, expired session, transaction limit exceeded, account restriction, suspicious transaction rule triggered, service provider downtime, or duplicate request prevention.

### 13.1 User Advice for Failed Payment

Support should tell users not to retry immediately if money was deducted, check wallet balance and bank statement, wait for auto reversal, save transaction screenshot, raise ticket with transaction ID, and not share OTP, PIN, or password.

## 14. Pending Transaction Policy

A pending transaction means PaySathi has not received final confirmation.

### 14.1 Pending State Causes

Pending states may be caused by bank response delay, merchant response delay, service provider confirmation delay, network timeout, settlement batch waiting, fraud monitoring hold, or external processor queue.

### 14.2 Pending Transaction Timeline

Most pending transactions are updated within 15 minutes to 24 hours. Some may require manual reconciliation and take 1 to 5 business days.

### 14.3 Support Action

Support should check transaction status, inform user not to retry if amount is deducted, create ticket if pending beyond normal time, escalate to Payment Operations if transaction is high value, and update user after final success, failure, or reversal.

## 15. KYC-Based Account Restriction Policy

PaySathi may restrict accounts if KYC is incomplete, KYC document is suspicious, multiple accounts use same document, user crosses risk threshold, user receives unusual number of transfers, user is reported for fraud, user violates transaction limits, regulatory screening flags the account, user refuses verification, or device/IP behavior is suspicious.

Restricted accounts may not be able to send money, withdraw to bank, pay merchants, receive high-value funds, change account details, close account, or use promotional rewards.

## 16. Suspicious Transaction and Fraud Policy

PaySathi monitors suspicious activity to protect users and comply with financial safety requirements.

### 16.1 Suspicious Activity Examples

Suspicious activity includes many small transactions to avoid limits, multiple accounts on same device, account used only for receiving unknown funds, sudden high-value transfers after KYC approval, frequent refund requests, login from unusual locations, repeated wrong PIN attempts, user reports unauthorized transaction, merchant receives abnormal payments, or wallet used for gambling, scams, illegal goods, or prohibited services.

### 16.2 Account Hold

If fraud is suspected, PaySathi may temporarily hold funds or restrict account features until review is complete.

### 16.3 User Cooperation

The user may be asked to provide identity document, source of funds, transaction purpose, relationship with sender or receiver, merchant invoice, bank statement, police report if required, and written declaration.

Failure to cooperate may result in extended restriction or account closure.

## 17. Unauthorized Transaction Policy

An unauthorized transaction is a transaction the user claims they did not perform.

### 17.1 Immediate User Action

The user should immediately change PaySathi PIN, log out from all devices, report transaction to PaySathi support, disable linked bank/card if needed, avoid sharing OTP or password, and file official complaint if fraud amount is high.

### 17.2 PaySathi Investigation

PaySathi may check device used, login time, IP address, OTP verification, PIN verification, transaction pattern, receiver details, KYC information, previous account activity, and whether user shared credentials.

### 17.3 Refund Eligibility

Refund is not guaranteed. PaySathi may deny refund if transaction was completed with correct PIN or OTP, user shared OTP/PIN/password/device access, user delayed reporting, receiver already withdrew funds, or evidence shows user authorized the transaction.

PaySathi may assist with account freezing, receiver investigation, and official report support where possible.

## 18. Merchant Dispute Policy

A merchant dispute occurs when the user paid a merchant but did not receive expected goods or services.

### 18.1 Common Merchant Disputes

Common merchant disputes include payment successful but order not created, merchant says payment not received, service not delivered, merchant cancelled order but refund not received, duplicate payment, wrong merchant charged, product not available after payment, merchant delivered wrong service, merchant refuses refund, and merchant account suspected fraud.

### 18.2 Required Information

User must submit PaySathi transaction ID, merchant name, order ID, amount, date and time, screenshot of payment receipt, screenshot of merchant order page, chat or email with merchant if available, and description of issue.

### 18.3 Investigation Timeline

1. Simple merchant confirmation: 24 to 48 business hours.
2. Duplicate payment: 1 to 3 business days.
3. Merchant refund approval: 3 to 7 business days.
4. Complex merchant dispute: up to 15 business days.

### 18.4 Merchant Non-Cooperation

If merchant does not respond, PaySathi may send repeated reminders, temporarily hold merchant settlement, escalate to Merchant Risk team, suspend merchant payment acceptance, provide transaction proof to user, and continue investigation based on available evidence.

## 19. Chargeback-Style Review Policy

Although wallet transactions may not always follow card-style chargeback rules, PaySathi may perform internal chargeback-style review for serious disputes.

Eligible cases may include unauthorized merchant payment, duplicate payment, technical failure, merchant not providing service, merchant fraud, and payment captured but order not generated.

Not eligible cases may include user changed mind, user selected wrong item, user gave wrong account number, user confirmed wrong mobile number, user shared OTP or PIN, or merchant delivered service according to terms.

## 20. Cashback and Reward Policy

PaySathi may offer cashback, promo codes, reward points, referral bonus, merchant discount, or campaign rewards.

### 20.1 Cashback Eligibility

Cashback may be provided only if user meets campaign terms, transaction is successful, transaction is not refunded, user does not violate fair-use policy, campaign quota is available, and user account is not restricted.

### 20.2 Cashback Reversal

PaySathi may reverse cashback if original transaction is refunded, user abuses campaign, duplicate account is detected, merchant cancels order, cashback was credited due to system error, or user violates PaySathi policy.

### 20.3 Cashback Delay

Cashback may take instant to 7 business days depending on campaign terms.

## 21. Wallet Balance and Account Closure Policy

A user may request account closure if KYC and account ownership are verified, no pending transactions exist, no active disputes exist, no legal or compliance hold exists, and wallet balance is withdrawn or transferred according to policy.

If wallet balance remains, PaySathi may ask the user to withdraw to a verified bank account before closure.

PaySathi may refuse account closure while investigation is ongoing.

## 22. Transaction Receipt and Verification Policy

Users can verify transactions through PaySathi app transaction history, transaction ID, downloadable receipt, SMS notification, email notification if enabled, merchant confirmation, and bank statement.

Support agents should always ask for transaction ID before giving transaction-specific status.

### 22.1 Receipt Contains

A PaySathi receipt may include transaction ID, date and time, sender, receiver or merchant, amount, fee if applicable, status, remarks, payment method, and reference number.

## 23. Support Ticket Priority

PaySathi classifies support tickets by severity.

### 23.1 Priority P1: Critical

Examples include large unauthorized transaction, system-wide payment outage, multiple users reporting failed deduction, active fraud attack, and merchant settlement issue affecting many users.

Target first response: Within 30 minutes.
Target resolution: Case dependent, urgent escalation required.

### 23.2 Priority P2: High

Examples include money deducted but not received, wallet-to-bank transfer delayed, merchant payment successful but order not created, KYC blocked for verified user, and account wrongly restricted.

Target first response: Within 4 business hours.
Target resolution: 1 to 5 business days.

### 23.3 Priority P3: Medium

Examples include KYC pending, cashback not received, limit exceeded explanation, receipt download issue, and profile update request.

Target first response: Within 24 business hours.
Target resolution: 2 to 7 business days.

### 23.4 Priority P4: Low

Examples include general inquiry, feature explanation, campaign information, app navigation help, and non-urgent feedback.

Target first response: Within 48 business hours.

## 24. Customer Communication Guidelines

Support agents must communicate clearly, respectfully, and in the user's preferred language where possible.

### 24.1 Good Support Response

A good response includes acknowledgement, current status, reason for delay or issue, required information, expected timeline, ticket number, and safety reminder.

### 24.2 Bad Support Response

Support agents must avoid "Wait garnus" without timeline, "Refund huncha" without checking, asking for OTP, blaming the user aggressively, giving technical jargon without explanation, closing ticket without final status, and telling user to pay again when money is deducted.

## 26. Internal Support Decision Tree

### Case: Money Deducted but Not Received

Step 1: Identify source of deduction. Bank, card, wallet, or merchant?

Step 2: Ask for transaction proof. Transaction ID, screenshot, reference number, amount, date/time.

Step 3: Check PaySathi transaction status. Successful, failed, pending, reversed, not found.

Step 4: Match with external reference. Bank reference, merchant order ID, service provider ID.

Step 5: Decide action. Credit wallet, wait for auto reversal, escalate to bank, escalate to merchant, or reject invalid claim.

Step 6: Communicate timeline. Give clear time estimate and ticket number.

## 27. Escalation Matrix

Payment Operations handles bank load mismatch, wallet-to-bank delay, pending transaction, reconciliation failure, and settlement mismatch.

Risk Operations handles fraud complaint, unauthorized transaction, suspicious receiver, account takeover, and high-value unusual transfer.

Compliance handles KYC rejection appeal, document mismatch, account restriction, regulatory hold, and source-of-funds verification.

Merchant Operations handles merchant payment dispute, merchant refund delay, merchant dashboard mismatch, merchant settlement issue, and merchant fraud report.

Technical Operations handles app error, OTP issue, API failure, notification delay, and login issue.

## 28. Required Ticket Fields

Every PaySathi support ticket must include ticket ID, registered mobile number, user name, issue category, transaction ID, amount, date and time, payment method, current status, user-provided evidence, support action taken, escalation team if any, expected resolution date, final outcome, and agent remarks.

## 29. User Safety Policy

PaySathi will never ask for OTP, wallet PIN, password, full card number, full bank login credentials, remote access to user device, screen sharing for sensitive payment actions, or payment to personal account for support service.

Users should report anyone pretending to be PaySathi support and asking for OTP, PIN, or money.

## 30. Final Notes for Users

Users are responsible for entering correct receiver details, reviewing confirmation screens, keeping login credentials secure, completing KYC honestly, and raising support tickets promptly.

PaySathi is responsible for maintaining secure payment infrastructure, investigating failed or disputed transactions, supporting users with clear communication, and reversing funds where PaySathi confirms technical failure or valid refund eligibility.

Refunds, reversals, and dispute outcomes depend on transaction status, merchant policy, bank response, service provider confirmation, user responsibility, and fraud investigation result.

This policy is designed to make support resolution fair, transparent, and traceable.
